Rationale

The previous existing COMP 1950 website was brief, but to the point and gets the job done providing BCIT students with weekly lecture notes, example, and assignments. Considering creating a more user-friendly interface design in focus on the user experience and interaction where lecture notes, examples and other information are easily accessed. Project team Lament reviewed and revised the existing web site using varieties of practices taught throughout this subjected site. The following below describes the results of this review and the implications for the recommended re-design.

The Audience

Targeted audiences are all BCIT students. For students who are currently enrolled in BCIT COMP 1950- Intermediate Web Development and Design. This web site will provide an easy access to lecture notes, assignments, and exercises in stride towards their career and academic future. Alumni students may gain access to review previous material while prospective students could have an overview or syllabus of the course potentials.

Site Layout

Keeping the web site stylish with innovative and inspiring idea without the use of lengthily flash intro, team Lament recommends and implicated a color schemes to create a more appealing web site to student. As well as altering the original navigation scheme and creating an additional navigation horizontally above, offering a more efficient access to content.

The site also upholds that of which is taught though out this course web site.

Site Navigation

“If it ain't broke, don't fix it.” The team has found that the existing vertical navigation is overall effective for visitors to navigate though the web site. But an addiction horizontal navigation and minor modification to the existing could render the site from good to great.

To enhanced user experience and interaction that allows students easier access to various components of each lecture. We have tweaked the content of the vertical navigation with a place for exercise, code examples, and other resources that is easily accessible and updateable. While including an addiction horizontal navigation at the top beneath the main banner, the site now offers a more efficient access to content.

Search Box

Search box will save the audience precious time making life easier finding their topic of interest. Rather than manually skimming though lecture sections, the team has made this tool available at the top navigation to assist visitors in finding relevant material.

External links

External links that are found frequently visited are made available to student on the side navigation under the subject title “Resources” for easier access. Although weekly lecture notes may often contain external links, team Lament believes that searching in the search box provided in the top navigation would preserve the sites’ elegance and prevent flooding of information that lacks content.
